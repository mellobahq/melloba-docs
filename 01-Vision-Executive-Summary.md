What Melloba is

Melloba is an AI-powered platform that helps parents understand and improve their everyday conversations with their young children — turning ordinary, everyday moments into deliberate development, privately and without judgment.

Tagline: Better conversations. Brighter futures.
Motto (internal decision test): Talk with, not just to.

The problem we exist to solve

The most formative environment in a child's early life is the ordinary back-and-forth conversation with the people who love them — and it is almost entirely unsupported. Parents receive no feedback and no visibility into the single most consequential thing they do. They are, in effect, flying blind through the most formative years of their child's life, flooded with contradictory advice optimized for guilt rather than for the child. Melloba exists to give parents gentle, private, evidence-based insight into their own conversations — so intention, the one thing that actually helps, gets a little support.

Why it matters (and the science, stated honestly)

The robust modern finding is not that children need to hear more words — it is that they need more genuine back-and-forth conversation. MIT/Harvard research (Romeo et al., 2018) found that the number of conversational turns predicted children's language ability and even the activity of a language-related brain region, independent of socioeconomic status and raw word count. As the lead researcher put it: "The important thing is not just to talk to your child, but to talk with your child."

We deliberately reject the popular "30 million word gap" (Hart & Risley, 1995). It has failed to replicate, rests on thin data, and carries a deficit framing that treats some families' speech as a depletion. This is a founding decision: we measure interaction quality (turns, responsiveness), not word volume, and we never tell a family they are behind.

Why now

Melloba could not have existed a few years ago. Reliable transcription of messy, real-world child-and-adult household speech has only recently become feasible; language models can now analyze interaction (turns, contingency, question type), not just words; personalization grounded in a specific family's real conversations has dropped to near-zero marginal cost; and privacy-preserving, closer-to-the-edge computation is maturing — letting us treat privacy as architecture, not decoration. The important caveat: capability is ready before trust, regulation, and social license are. We move at the speed of earned trust, which is slower than the speed of technology.

What kind of company we intend to be

A company that would rather grow slowly and be trusted than grow fast and be feared. Over ten years: first, earn the right to listen by being genuinely useful to a small number of families; then become the private, parent-controlled record of a child's conversational development; eventually, earn enough institutional trust that clinicians and educators regard our insight as legitimate — potentially helping surface developmental concerns early, but never diagnosing casually or before we can do so responsibly.

What we will never do

No advertising model, ever. No selling or brokering family data. No surveillance framing — Melloba is not a tool to monitor or police children. No scores or rankings that reduce a child to a number. No gamification, guilt loops, or engagement dark patterns. No medical claims we aren't qualified to make. The general rule: if a feature makes money by making families anxious, competitive, watched, or exposed, we don't build it — regardless of the revenue.

How we define success

We hold two scorecards. The mission scorecard asks whether families converse more richly, feel less anxious (a rise in anxiety is treated as failure), trust us durably, and — where measurable — whether children benefit. The business scorecard tracks predictable recurring revenue from families who'd be genuinely sorry to lose us. When they conflict, the mission wins and we find another way to make the business work. We intend to build a durable, valuable business at real scale — we simply refuse the forbidden paths to get there.

North Star Metric: the number of families showing sustained improvement in the quality of their conversations with their child (genuine back-and-forth engagement), with a mandatory guardrail that parental anxiety must not rise. Downloads, MAU/DAU, and revenue are watched but explicitly rejected as measures of success, because a child-conversation product can inflate all of them by manufacturing anxiety and dependence.

The honest risks

Measuring child development is scientifically hard and harmful to get wrong — so early on we describe and coach rather than diagnose or score. Recording children's audio is a real regulatory and trust minefield (COPPA, GDPR, India's DPDP Act), so privacy-by-architecture is an engineering requirement, not an afterthought — and it sits in tension with the current default stack (Firebase/OpenAI/Whisper), a tension we resolve deliberately, not by default.

Values, in one line each

Talk with, not just to — engage, don't broadcast. Trust before scale — trust is the product. Honest about what we don't know — intellectual honesty as a safety feature. Restraint is a feature — not building is often the right call. Build for the child, then for the decade — optimize for the beneficiary and for durability.
